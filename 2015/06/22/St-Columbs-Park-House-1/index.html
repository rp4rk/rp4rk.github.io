St. Columbs Park House

Project Information

St. Columbs Park House is a peace and reconciliation centre located in Derry, Northern Ireland. Across the Lagan from the city centre, the house was described as a hidden gem in the context of what the city has to offer. The house required rebranding, and as part of the process a new website was desired.



Project Role

My primary roles in the project were web development, graphic design, and photographer. I was also responsible for creating a mutually acceptable brief between the team and client, including defining the project scope and acquiring further information to understand the organization and their goals.

I was solely responsible for identifying which features were needed from the new website, and the most suitable platform for meeting these needs.








Before and After

Logo & Print Deliverables

The old logo had to be updated, I developed the core logo layout - with the team illustrator providing enhanced designs to be contained. This new logo functioned well not only as an identifying brand, but also was modifiable for the wide array of events the park house would be host to.

Once the new logo and by-line were set in stone, I was responsible for communicating with print shops in order to acquire accurate specifications to create signage, letterheads, and more merchandise.





Web Development

The website was built using SASS and Wordpress. The build tools involved in the creation were bower, grunt, and the SCSS grunt plugin to compile SCSS into browser readable CSS.
